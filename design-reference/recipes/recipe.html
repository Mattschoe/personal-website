Brown-Butter Chocolate Chip Cookies

Browning the butter takes ten extra minutes and turns an ordinary cookie into the one people ask you to bring. Deeply nutty, chewy in the middle, crisp at the edge.

Time45 min
Makes18
EffortEasy

Ingredients

  • 230 gunsalted butter
  • 200 gdark brown sugar
  • 100 gcaster sugar
  • 2eggs, plus 1 yolk
  • 2 tspvanilla extract
  • 300 gplain flour
  • 1 tspbaking soda
  • 1 tspflaky sea salt
  • 250 gdark chocolate, chopped

Method

  1. Melt the butter in a light-bottomed pan over medium heat. Keep swirling — it will foam, then quiet down and smell nutty as the milk solids turn golden brown. Pour into a bowl and cool for 10 minutes.

  2. Whisk both sugars into the warm butter, then beat in the eggs, extra yolk, and vanilla until glossy and slightly thickened, about a minute.

  3. Fold in the flour, baking soda, and salt until just combined, then the chocolate. Chill the dough for at least 30 minutes (overnight is better).

  4. Scoop into golf-ball portions onto lined trays, leaving room to spread. Bake at 180°C for 11–13 minutes, until the edges are set but the centres look underdone.

  5. Bang the tray on the counter once out of the oven for craggy edges. Finish with extra flaky salt and cool on the tray for 5 minutes.

Matt's note

Chilling overnight is the single biggest upgrade here — the flour hydrates and the brown-butter flavour deepens. If you can wait, do.
